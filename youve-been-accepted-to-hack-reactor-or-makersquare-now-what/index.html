You've been accepted to Hack Reactor or MakerSquare. Now what?

So you've received your acceptance email from a Hack Reactor Core School and you have a few weeks before your start date.

If you're more than a month out from your start date, I'm sure you're not even thinking about studying or finishing the prework just yet. If you're a few weeks out from your start date, you're probably thinking you have plenty of time to finish the prework and to study whatever you'd like before your cohort starts. And if you start next week, I'm sure prework is the only thing on your mind right now.

In this post, I hope to give you a basic list on what to study before starting your immersive experience.

"Truly great creative achievement require hundreds, if not thousands, of hours of work, and we have to make time every single day to put in those hours."

I have no doubt that you'll break hundreds of hours of coding and studying during your time at a Hack Reactor Core School. However, my job is to get you into that habit earlier with this list and therefore, get you into the thousands of hours of work sooner.

Familiarity with the following before you start will make all the difference.

What to study

Let me preface this part by saying finish your prework before even looking at this list. There's a reason they assign it and require that you finish it before starting.

Now for the resources.

Know this:

  • JavaScript - This should go without saying. I know you can't know the language inside out by the time you start (I mean part of the reason why you're going through an immersive program is to learn this), but the more you know about the little quirks of the language, the better position you'll be in to tackle the challenges in the program. I recommend reading through Kyle Simpson's book series YDKJS.
  • Data Structures - What's a data structure? It's a way data can be organized so it can be used more efficiently. Spend time learning what Stacks, Queues, Linked Lists, Trees, Graphs, and Hash Tables are.
  • Time Complexity - This will come up time and time again not only in the program, but also in technical interviews. Spend the time to familiarize yourself with this as soon as you can. Know what Big O notation is and articulating a runtime for a certain algorithm.
  • Sorting Algorithms - Speaking of algorithms, know the different types of sorting algorithms out there as well as the time complexity for each.

Sign up for this:

  • Leetcode - A great resource to practice coding challenges.
  • Frontend Dev Weekly
  • JavaScript Weekly
  • Node Weekly

Buy this:

  • Cracking The Coding Interview by Gayle Laakmann McDowell - This will not only come in handy when you finish the program and are interviewing, but there's also some great explanations in the earlier chapters on time complexity.

If you found any other resource helpful, feel free to reach out and I will update this list for future students.
